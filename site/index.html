01 — Practice

What I work on.

A short note on the work — what I do, where, and how the pieces tend to come together.
“

My practice has three sides that feed each other. I play the flute — chamber music, recitals and the occasional orchestral seat. I write pieces, mostly for chamber forces, and lately more for stage works that combine music, projection and design. And I run two organisations: CreArtBox in New York and Festival ADAR in rural Asturias.

Most projects start as something practical — a venue, a programme, a deadline — and the composing follows from that. I tend to design my own visuals and lighting too, because the music and the room usually want to be thought about together. The day-to-day is grant writing, rehearsing, editing video, and patient logistics with collaborators on two continents.

— G. L., New York · 2025

05 — Leadership

Organizations & fundraising.

Founder and director of two arts organizations across two countries. Sustained institutional support since 2013.

CreArtBox

2013 – Present · NYC
Executive Director · Co-Founder · Co-Artistic Director

Co-founded with pianist Josefina Urraca. Built from a small ensemble into a nationally recognised nonprofit with 20+ professional artists and 20+ performances per year at venues including The DiMenna Center, Culture Lab Queens, Mark Morris Center, The William Vale, Greenwich House, NY Society for Ethical Culture, Brooklyn Public Library and Queens Library Culture Pass. Artist in Residence at CultureLab, Queens. Sustained NYSCA support; recognised by The New Yorker, Broadway World and Time Out. Origins trace to Cre.Art Project, founded in Spain in 2006 with Tagore González.

Visit site ↗

Festival ADAR

2020 – Present · Asturias, Spain
Executive Director · Co-Founder · Co-Artistic Director

Founded the Association for the Development of the Arts in Rural Areas (ADAR). Directs an itinerant arts festival bringing world-class classical music, artist talks, composer commissions, visual installations and multimedia collaborations to underserved rural communities in Spain. Includes a site-specific light and projection installation at the Capilla de los Dolores in Grado, Asturias.

Fundraising

Across CreArtBox (NYC) and Festival ADAR (Spain), I have raised sustained operating, programming and project funding from public agencies and private foundations on both sides of the Atlantic. Awards have included multi-year support from the New York State Council on the Arts, the National Endowment for the Arts, the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs and the Government of the Principality of Asturias, alongside grants and recognitions from the Aaron Copland Fund for Music, the Amphion Foundation, Mathis-Pfohl Foundation, Tri-M Foundation, Queens Council on the Arts, Flushing Town Hall, the Spanish Embassy and others. The work has been supported continuously since 2013, allowing both organisations to grow a programming season, commission new work and reach audiences in NYC and rural Spain.

08 — About

Bio & press kit.

Short and long bio for press use. Headshots and CV available below.

Guillermo Laporta is a flutist, composer and cultural producer based in New York City and Asturias, Spain. He is co-founder and Executive Director of CreArtBox (NYC, 2013) and Festival ADAR (Spain, 2020), and works across chamber music, opera, multimedia performance and film.

Trained at the Royal College of Music in London and Musikene in San Sebastián, he has performed with the Oviedo Filarmonía and New York International Chamber Orchestra, and shared the stage with Sir Roger Norrington, Vladimir Ashkenazy and Truls Mørk. His original work has been featured in The New Yorker, Time Out, El País and I Care if You Listen.

Guillermo Laporta (b. Asturias, Spain) is a flutist, composer, designer and cultural producer based between New York City and Spain. His practice connects classical and contemporary chamber music with theatre, dance, video art and immersive design — what he and his collaborators call the visual concert.

He co-founded CreArtBox in 2013 with pianist Josefina Urraca, building a New York-based nonprofit ensemble of 20+ professional artists with a 20+ performance season at venues including The DiMenna Center, Mark Morris Center, The William Vale, Greenwich House and the New York Society for Ethical Culture. CreArtBox has received sustained support from the New York State Council on the Arts and recognition from The New Yorker, Broadway World and Time Out.

In 2020 he founded the Association for the Development of the Arts in Rural Areas (ADAR) in Asturias and directs an itinerant festival bringing world-class chamber music, composer commissions and visual installations to underserved rural communities — supported by the Government of Asturias with €120,000 over four awards (2021–25).

As a flutist Laporta served as Co-Principal Flute of the Oviedo Filarmonía (2009–12), Principal Flute of the Herald Chamber Orchestra (2012–15) and the New York International Chamber Orchestra (2013–18). He has performed with the BBC Orchestra, Orquesta del Principado de Asturias, Le Train Bleu Ensemble and Orquesta Sinfónica de Euskadi, and recorded for Warner, EMI and Naxos.

His original creative work includes the opera-ballet-films Architecture of a Common Man (2023) and Two Roads (2019, a New Yorker Critics' Pick), the multimedia chamber piece Queens Preludes (2021, toured to NYC, Madrid and Tokyo), the visual concert trilogy Fragile Form (2022), and AWAVE (2018–19), a solo album and immersive video installation supported by the Spanish Embassy and the Amphion Foundation.

He holds an M.Mus. in Advanced Performance from the Royal College of Music, London (Jaime Martín, Paul Edmund Davies, Sue Thomas), an M.A. in Arts Management from Alcalá de Henares University, and a Superior Degree in Music from Musikene, San Sebastián.
